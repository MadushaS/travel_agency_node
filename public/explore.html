Surfing in Sri Lanka

Where to Surf in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is home to some of the best surfing spots in the world, with a variety of breaks to suit all levels of surfers.

When to Surf in Sri Lanka

The best time for surfing in Sri Lanka is from November to April when the southwest monsoon brings in consistent waves to the west and south coast of Sri Lanka. During this time, the water is also warmer and clearer, making it ideal for surfing. The east coast has a different surf season and the best time to surf in the east coast of Sri Lanka is from May to September.

Top surfing spots in Sri Lanka

Arugam Bay

Located on the east coast of Sri Lanka, Arugam Bay is a world-renowned surf spot that's popular with advanced surfers. The best time to surf here is from May to November

Hikkaduwa

Hikkaduwa is a popular surfing destination located on the west coast of Sri Lanka. This popular beach town on the west coast of Sri Lanka has some great surf breaks for beginners and intermediate surfers. The best time to surf here is from November to April.

Weligama

Weligama is another popular surfing destination located on the south coast of Sri Lanka. The best time to surf in Weligama is from November to April. Weligama is known for its long, gentle waves, making it a great place for beginners.

Mirissa

Mirissa is a picturesque beach town on the south coast of Sri Lanka that's known for its surf breaks. The best time to surf here is from November to April.

Unawatuna

Unawatuna is a small town located on the south coast of Sri Lanka. It is a great place for beginner and intermediate surfers. The best time to surf in Unawatuna is from November to April.

Safari in Sri Lanka

Safari in Sri Lanka

The best time to go on a safari in Sri Lanka is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this time, the animals congregate around water sources, making them easier to spot. However, if you're looking to see elephants, the best time to go is from August to October, when they gather in large herds.

Where to Go

Sri Lanka has several national parks and wildlife reserves where you can go on a safari. Here are some of the most popular ones:

Yala National Park

Yala National Park is the most popular national park in Sri Lanka, and it's home to a variety of animals, including elephants, leopards, sloth bears, and crocodiles. The park is divided into several zones, and each zone has its own attractions.

Udawalawe National Park

Udawalawe National Park is known for its large population of elephants. The park is also home to several other animals, including water buffalo, deer, and crocodiles.

Wilpattu National Park

Wilpattu National Park is the largest national park in Sri Lanka, and it's home to a variety of animals, including leopards, elephants, sloth bears, and water buffalo.

Minneriya National Park

Minneriya National Park is known for its large population of elephants. The park is also home to several other animals, including deer, monkeys, and crocodiles.

How to Plan Your Safari

There are several tour operators that offer safari packages in Sri Lanka. You can either book a private safari or join a group tour. Private safaris are more expensive but offer a more personalized experience, while group tours are more affordable but less flexible.

What to Bring

Here are some things you should bring on your safari:

  • Water and snacks to keep you hydrated and energized.
  • Sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Insect repellent to protect yourself from mosquitoes and other bugs.
  • Binoculars to get a closer look at the animals.
  • Camera to capture the memories.
  • Comfortable shoes and clothing.

Safari Tips

Here are some tips to help you make the most of your safari:

  • Listen to your guide and follow their instructions.
  • Keep a safe distance from the animals and never approach them.
  • Stay quiet and avoid making loud noises that might scare the animals away.
  • Be patient and don't expect to see all the animals in one go.
  • Respect the animals and their natural habitats.
Hiking in Sri Lanka

Hiking in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is a country blessed with natural beauty and diverse landscapes, making it an ideal destination for hiking and trekking enthusiasts. Here's a guide to help you plan your hiking adventure in Sri Lanka.

Where to Hike in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka has a variety of hiking trails to suit all levels of hikers. Here are some popular hiking destinations:

Adam's Peak

Adam's Peak, also known as Sri Pada, is a sacred mountain located in the central highlands of Sri Lanka. The hike to the top of Adam's Peak is a popular pilgrimage for people of all faiths, and the stunning views from the summit make it well worth the effort.

Horton Plains National Park

Horton Plains National Park is a protected area in the central highlands of Sri Lanka. The park is home to a variety of wildlife and plant species and has some stunning hiking trails that take you through forests, grasslands, and cloud forests. The most popular hike in Horton Plains is to the World's End viewpoint, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Ella

Ella is a small town located in the central highlands of Sri Lanka and is a popular destination for hikers and nature lovers. The hike to Ella Rock takes you through tea plantations, forests, and villages, and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

The Knuckles Mountain Range

The Knuckles Mountain Range is a protected area in the central highlands of Sri Lanka and is home to a variety of endemic plant and animal species. The range offers some of the best hiking trails in Sri Lanka, ranging from easy walks to challenging treks.

When to hike in Sri Lanka

The best time to hike in Sri Lanka is from December to March when the weather is dry and cool, making it ideal for outdoor activities. However, some hiking destinations, such as Horton Plains, can be visited year-round.

Scuba Diving and Whale Watching in Sri Lanka

Scuba Diving and Whale Watching

Sri Lanka is a great destination for scuba diving and whale watching. The island nation has an abundance of marine life and a variety of diving sites to suit all levels of divers. The best time for diving and whale watching in Sri Lanka is from November to April, when the sea is calm and visibility is good.

Scuba Diving in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka's coral reefs, shipwrecks, and abundant marine life make it an excellent destination for scuba diving. Here are some of the best diving sites in Sri Lanka:

Pigeon Island

Pigeon Island is located off the east coast of Sri Lanka and is a marine national park. The island is known for its coral reefs and a variety of marine life, including reef sharks, turtles, and barracudas.

Hikkaduwa

Hikkaduwa is located on the west coast of Sri Lanka and is one of the most popular diving spots in the country. The area is home to a variety of marine life, including schools of colorful fish, reef sharks, and sea turtles.

Trincomalee

Trincomalee is located on the east coast of Sri Lanka and is another popular diving destination. The area is known for its coral reefs, shipwrecks, and whale sharks.

Kirinda

Kirinda is located on the south coast of Sri Lanka and is known for its underwater caves, shipwrecks, and a variety of marine life, including barracudas and groupers.

Whale Watching in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is one of the best destinations in the world for whale watching, with several species of whales and dolphins found in the waters around the island. Here are some of the best places to go whale watching in Sri Lanka:

Mirissa

Mirissa is located on the south coast of Sri Lanka and is one of the best places to see blue whales. Other species of whales and dolphins can also be seen here.

Trincomalee

Trincomalee is located on the east coast of Sri Lanka and is another popular whale watching destination. Blue whales, sperm whales, and spinner dolphins can be seen here.

Kalpitiya

Kalpitiya is located on the west coast of Sri Lanka and is a great place to see dolphins, including the playful spinner dolphins.

Tips for Scuba Diving in Sri Lanka:

Here are some tips to help you make the most of your scuba diving experience:

  • Choose a reputable dive center: Look for a dive center with experienced instructors and good reviews. Make sure they follow safety protocols and have well-maintained equipment.
  • Check the weather conditions: Before you plan your dive, check the weather forecast to make sure the conditions are suitable. Strong winds and heavy rain can affect visibility and make diving dangerous.
  • Be aware of currents: Sri Lanka has strong ocean currents, so it's important to be aware of them when diving. Make sure you have the proper training and experience to handle these conditions.
  • Respect the marine life: As a diver, it's important to respect the marine life and their habitat. Do not touch or disturb the marine animals, and avoid damaging coral reefs.
  • Follow the dive plan: Before you go on your dive, make sure you have a dive plan and stick to it. This will help ensure that you stay safe and avoid any potential risks.

Favourite Destinations

Galle

Galle is a city on the southwest coast of Sri Lanka. Its known for Galle Fort, the fortified old city founded by Portuguese colonists in the 16th century. Stone sea walls, expanded by the Dutch, encircle car-free streets with architecture reflecting Portuguese, Dutch and British rule. Notable buildings include the 18th-century Dutch Reformed Church. Galle Lighthouse stands on the forts southeast tip.

See Info

Colombo

Colombo, the capital of Sri Lanka, has a long history as a port on ancient east-west trade routes, ruled successively by the Portuguese, Dutch and British. That heritage is reflected in its its architecture, mixing colonial buildings with high-rises and shopping malls. The imposing Colombo National Museum, dedicated to Sri Lankan history, borders sprawling Viharamahadevi Park and its giant Buddha.

See Info

Anuradapura

Anuradhapura is an ancient city, located at a distance of 205 km from Colombo in Sri Lanka. The city is famous for its well-preserved ruins of ancient Sri Lankan civilization. It was the third capital of the Kingdom of Rajarata, following the kingdoms of Tambapanni and Upatissa Nuwara.
