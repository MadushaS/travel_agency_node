Galle, Sri Lanka

Galle is a coastal city in the southwest of Sri Lanka. The city is known for its historic Galle Fort, beautiful beaches, and delicious food. It's a popular destination for tourists looking for a relaxing getaway.

Here's a guide to everything you need to know about Galle:

Places to Visit

Galle Fort

The Galle Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most popular attractions in Sri Lanka. It was built by the Portuguese in the 16th century and later fortified by the Dutch. The fort is a great place to take a stroll and admire the colonial architecture. There are also several museums and art galleries within the fort that are worth a visit. It features old colonial-style buildings, narrow streets, and ramparts that overlook the ocean.

Dutch Reformed Church

The Dutch Reformed Church is a historic colonial-era church, featuring Dutch and British architectural styles church located in the heart of Galle Fort. The church is known for its beautiful architecture and stained glass windows.

National Maritime Museum

The National Maritime Museum is a museum dedicated to the history of Sri Lanka's maritime heritage. It's located in Galle Fort and houses artifacts from the Dutch and British colonial periods. The museum is a great place to learn about the country's history and culture.

Galle Lighthouse

The Galle Lighthouse is a historic lighthouse built by the British, located on the ramparts of the Galle Fort, offering scenic views of the surrounding area.

Meera Mosque

A historic mosque located within the Galle Fort, built by Muslim traders from the Arabian Peninsula in the 19th century.

Japanese Peace Pagoda

A Buddhist temple located on top of Rumassala Hill, offering panoramic views of the ocean and surrounding area.

Koggala lake

Koggala lake is a beautiful lake that's perfect for swimming and sunbathing. It's located just a few kilometers away from Galle and is easily accessible by tuk-tuk or taxi. There are also several restaurants and bars along the lake where you can grab a drink or a bite to eat.

Unawatuna Beach

Unawatuna Beach is a beautiful crescent-shaped beach that's perfect for swimming and sunbathing. It's located just a few kilometers away from Galle and is easily accessible by tuk-tuk or taxi. There are also several restaurants and bars along the beach where you can grab a drink or a bite to eat.

Jungle Beach

Jungle Beach is a secluded beach that's perfect for swimming and snorkeling. It's located just a few kilometers away from Galle and is easily accessible by tuk-tuk or taxi. There are also several restaurants and bars along the beach where you can grab a drink or a bite to eat.
