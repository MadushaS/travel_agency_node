Galle, Sri Lanka

Galle is a coastal city in the southwest of Sri Lanka. The city is known for its historic Galle Fort, beautiful beaches, and delicious food. It's a popular destination for tourists looking for a relaxing getaway.

Here's a guide to everything you need to know about Galle:

Places to Visit

Galle Fort

The Galle Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most popular attractions in Sri Lanka. It was built by the Portuguese in the 16th century and later fortified by the Dutch. The fort is a great place to take a stroll and admire the colonial architecture. There are also several museums and art galleries within the fort that are worth a visit. It features old colonial-style buildings, narrow streets, and ramparts that overlook the ocean.

Dutch Reformed Church

The Dutch Reformed Church is a historic colonial-era church, featuring Dutch and British architectural styles church located in the heart of Galle Fort. The church is known for its beautiful architecture and stained glass windows.

National Maritime Museum

The National Maritime Museum is a museum dedicated to the history of Sri Lanka's maritime heritage. It's located in Galle Fort and houses artifacts from the Dutch and British colonial periods. The museum is a great place to learn about the country's history and culture.

Galle Lighthouse

The Galle Lighthouse is a historic lighthouse built by the British, located on the ramparts of the Galle Fort, offering scenic views of the surrounding area.

Meera Mosque

A historic mosque located within the Galle Fort, built by Muslim traders from the Arabian Peninsula in the 19th century.

Japanese Peace Pagoda

A Buddhist temple located on top of Rumassala Hill, offering panoramic views of the ocean and surrounding area.

Koggala lake

Koggala lake is a beautiful lake that's perfect for swimming and sunbathing. It's located just a few kilometers away from Galle and is easily accessible by tuk-tuk or taxi. There are also several restaurants and bars along the lake where you can grab a drink or a bite to eat.

Unawatuna Beach

Unawatuna Beach is a beautiful crescent-shaped beach that's perfect for swimming and sunbathing. It's located just a few kilometers away from Galle and is easily accessible by tuk-tuk or taxi. There are also several restaurants and bars along the beach where you can grab a drink or a bite to eat.

Jungle Beach

Jungle Beach is a secluded beach that's perfect for swimming and snorkeling. It's located just a few kilometers away from Galle and is easily accessible by tuk-tuk or taxi. There are also several restaurants and bars along the beach where you can grab a drink or a bite to eat.

Activities

Immerse Yourself in Cultural Delights

Take a Tuk-Tuk Safari

Embark on a thrilling tuk-tuk safari to explore the countryside and rural villages surrounding Galle. Enjoy the scenic landscapes, interact with locals, and get a glimpse of the authentic Sri Lankan way of life.

Visit a Sea Turtle Hatchery

Get up close and personal with these fascinating marine creatures by visiting a sea turtle hatchery. Learn about conservation efforts, witness baby turtles being released into the ocean, and even have the chance to participate in feeding sessions.

Try Stilt Fishing

Experience the traditional fishing technique of stilt fishing, where fishermen perch on wooden stilts in the water and cast their lines. Capture this unique sight and even try your hand at fishing with the guidance of local fishermen.

Attend a Traditional Mask Workshop

Immerse yourself in the rich cultural heritage of Sri Lanka by attending a traditional mask workshop. Learn about the intricate craftsmanship involved in making these vibrant masks and try your hand at creating your own masterpiece.

Participate in a Traditional Sri Lankan Cooking Class

Dive into the culinary delights of Sri Lanka by joining a traditional cooking class. Learn to prepare authentic Sri Lankan dishes using fresh ingredients and traditional cooking methods. Enjoy the satisfaction of indulging in your own creations afterward.

Attend a Traditional Sri Lankan Dance Performance

Immerse yourself in the vibrant culture of Sri Lanka by attending a traditional dance performance. Watch skilled dancers perform traditional dance forms, accompanied by lively music and colorful costumes. Get a glimpse of the country's rich artistic traditions.

Shopping

Discover Treasures in Galle: A Shopper's Paradise!

Galle Fort Shopping

Explore the charming streets of Galle Fort, lined with boutique shops and art galleries. Discover unique handicrafts, antiques, jewelry, clothing, and souvenirs crafted by local artisans.

Barefoot

Visit Barefoot, a renowned Sri Lankan brand known for its handloom textiles, clothing, and accessories. Browse through their colorful collection of vibrant sarongs, scarves, bags, and home decor items.

Stick No Bills

Step into Stick No Bills, a gallery and store dedicated to vintage-inspired travel posters and postcards. Find beautifully designed prints featuring iconic Sri Lankan landmarks and retro travel aesthetics.

Laksala

Explore Laksala, a government-owned handicrafts emporium that showcases a wide range of traditional Sri Lankan crafts. Shop for batik textiles, wooden carvings, pottery, gemstones, and other authentic local products.

Pedlar's Street

Wander along Pedlar's Street, a bustling shopping street within Galle Fort. Discover boutique stores, art galleries, and souvenir shops offering a variety of handmade crafts, clothing, and accessories.

Spa Ceylon

Indulge in the luxurious world of Spa Ceylon, a local brand specializing in Ayurvedic-inspired beauty and wellness products. Explore their range of aromatic oils, body care products, teas, and spa accessories.

Maritime Archeological Museum Shop

Visit the Maritime Archeological Museum Shop located within Galle Fort. Browse through their collection of maritime-themed books, maps, nautical antiques, and replicas of ancient artifacts.

Moonstone Mines

Take a trip to the nearby village of Meetiyagoda, famous for its moonstone mines. Visit the local shops and gemstone showrooms to find beautiful moonstone jewelry and gemstones directly sourced from the mines.
